Theory & Mathematical Models

1. Introduction to the Low-Temperature Solar Stirling Engine

The direct conversion of solar power into mechanical power reduces both the cost and complexity of the prime mover. The model simulated here represents a prototype of a solar-powered Stirling engine designed to operate at a low temperature range.

2. Solar Radiation Modeling on Tilted Surfaces

Global solar radiation on a tilted surface is the algebraic sum of three distinct solar components: direct (beam) solar radiation, diffuse solar radiation, and ground-reflected solar radiation.

$$G_t = G_{B_t} + G_{D_t} + G_{R_t}$$

Beam Radiation

The beam component is calculated using the beam radiation on a horizontal surface ($G_B$) and a geometric multiplier known as the beam radiation tilt factor ($R_B$).

$$R_B = \frac{\cos(\theta)}{\cos(\Phi)} = \frac{\sin(L - \beta)\sin(\delta) + \cos(L - \beta)\cos(\delta)\cos(h)}{\sin(L)\sin(\delta) + \cos(L)\cos(\delta)\cos(h)}$$
$$G_{B_t} = G_B R_B$$

Diffuse and Ground-Reflected Radiation

Utilizing the Liu and Jordan Isotropic Model:

$$G_{D_t} = (0.11 \times G_{B_n}) \left( \frac{1 + \cos(\beta)}{2} \right)$$
$$G_{R_t} = (G_B + G_D) \rho \left( \frac{1 - \cos(\beta)}{2} \right)$$

3. Thermal Analysis & Energy Balance

To determine output power, a model for thermal analysis is required. This considers radiation and convection heat transfer between the absorber, the working fluid, and the lower plate.

Energy Balance at the Hot Absorber (Plate 1):

$$ \alpha_{p1} G_t + h_{c,a1-p1}(T_{a1} - T_{p1}) + \sigma \epsilon_{p1} F_{a1-p1}(T_{a1}^4 - T_{p1}^4) = h_{c,p1-air}(T_{p1} - T_{air}) + \sigma \epsilon_{p1} F_{p1-p2}(T_{p1}^4 - T_{p2}^4) $$

Energy Balance at the Cold Sink (Plate 2):

$$ h_{c,air-p2}(T_{air} - T_{p2}) + \sigma \epsilon_{p1} F_{p1-p2} \left(\frac{A_{p1}}{A_{p2}}\right) (T_{p1}^4 - T_{p2}^4) = h_{c,p2-a2}(T_{p2} - T_{a2}) + \sigma \epsilon_{p2} F_{p2-a2}(T_{p2}^4 - T_{a2}^4) $$

Energy Balance of the Working Fluid:

$$ h_{c,p1-air}(T_{p1} - T_{air}) = h_{c,air-p2}(T_{air} - T_{p2}) $$
